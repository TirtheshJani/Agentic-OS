# Substack hook patterns

Short reference for the opening of a Substack post. The hook is the
first two or three sentences; it decides whether the reader scrolls or
closes the tab. Pair this with `assets/post-template.md` (the "Why
this, why now" section is where the hook lives).

## Four hook shapes that work

1. **Concrete moment.** Drop the reader inside a specific event. Date,
   place, what happened. No abstract framing yet.
   - "Last Tuesday I watched a junior engineer push to main, break
     prod, and recover the system in seven minutes. Here is what that
     workflow looked like."

2. **Sharp claim.** State a counter-intuitive position in one sentence,
   then promise the argument.
   - "Most retrieval pipelines are slower than a SQL `LIKE`. The
     reason is structural."

3. **One number.** A single piece of data the reader did not know.
   Follow with the implication.
   - "Eighty-three percent of the agent runs in our logs touch the
     same three tools. That changes how you should price tool calls."

4. **The question the reader already has.** Name the thing the reader
   has been wondering, then say you will answer it.
   - "Why does every agent demo work in the keynote and fail in
     production? It is not the model."

## What to avoid

Cross-reference `skills/content/avoid-ai-writing/SKILL.md` for the full
list. The hooks most flagged on Substack drafts:

- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape of ..." — formulaic opening.
- "Let's dive in" / "Let's explore" — chatbot artifact.
- "What if I told you ..." — engagement-bait framing.
- Generic rhetorical questions with no setup.
- Opening with the writer's emotion ("I was excited to discover ...").

## Length

Two to four sentences. If the hook runs past five, the reader has
already done the work the body should do. Cut.

## TL;DR vs. hook

The post template separates them deliberately. The TL;DR is the post
in three sentences for someone who will not read further. The hook is
the reason a reader who is on the fence stays. Do not let them
collapse into the same paragraph.
